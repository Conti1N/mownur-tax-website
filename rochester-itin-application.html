What Is an ITIN

File Taxes Without
a Social Security Number

An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a nine-digit number issued by the IRS for people who are required to have a federal tax identification number but don't qualify for a Social Security Number.

This includes undocumented immigrants, foreign nationals, and their dependents. Having an ITIN lets you file taxes, claim tax credits, and build your tax history — regardless of immigration status.

We are IRS Certifying Acceptance Agents (CAA), which means we can verify your identity documents on-site. You don't need to send your passport to the IRS.

Who Needs an ITIN

  • Non-resident aliensRequired to file a U.S. tax return or claim a treaty benefit.
  • Resident aliensFiling based on days present in the U.S. who don't qualify for an SSN.
  • Dependents of U.S. citizensSpouses or children who are non-residents and can't get an SSN.
  • Students and visa holdersF, J, or M visa holders earning income in the U.S.
  • Undocumented immigrantsAnyone required to file taxes but ineligible for an SSN.

The Process

How We Get You Your ITIN

01
Eligibility Check

We review your situation to confirm you need an ITIN and which documents you must provide.

As a Certifying Acceptance Agent, we verify your passport or ID on-site — no mailing originals to the IRS.

04

The IRS typically issues ITINs within 7–11 weeks. We track your application and notify you when it arrives.
