Can I File Taxes Without a Social Security Number?

March 2025 By Mownur Tax Services Rochester, MN

If you live and work in the United States but don't have a Social Security Number (SSN), you may be wondering whether you're required to file taxes — and if so, how. The answer to both questions is yes: you can file U.S. taxes without an SSN, and in many cases you're legally required to.

The tool that makes this possible is called an ITIN — Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. Issued by the IRS, it's specifically designed for people who have a U.S. tax filing obligation but are not eligible for a Social Security Number.

Who Needs an ITIN?

You may need an ITIN if you fall into one of these categories:

  • Undocumented immigrants who earn income in the U.S. and are required to file a federal tax return
  • Non-resident aliens who have a U.S. tax filing requirement
  • Foreign nationals who are not eligible for an SSN but receive U.S. income
  • Dependents or spouses of U.S. citizens or resident aliens who are not eligible for an SSN

Important: Having an ITIN does not authorize you to work in the U.S. or affect your immigration status in any way. It is solely a tax processing number. However, filing taxes with an ITIN creates a record that can be valuable in future immigration proceedings.

What Can You Do With an ITIN?

With an ITIN, you can:

  • File a federal tax return (and be required to)
  • Claim tax credits like the Child Tax Credit (for dependents with SSNs) and the Other Dependent Credit
  • Open certain types of bank accounts
  • Apply for certain state-issued licenses

Note: You cannot use an ITIN to claim the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) — that credit requires a valid SSN for yourself, your spouse, and any qualifying children.

What Documents Do You Need to Apply for an ITIN?

To apply for an ITIN, you must submit Form W-7 along with proof of identity and foreign status. The IRS requires original documents or certified copies. The most commonly accepted single document is a valid passport. If you don't have a passport, the IRS accepts a combination of other documents such as a national ID card, civil birth certificate, and a U.S. driver's license.

Why Apply Through a Certifying Acceptance Agent?

When you apply for an ITIN on your own, you must mail your original documents (such as your passport) to the IRS — which means being without your documents for weeks. A safer option is to work with an IRS-authorized Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA).

As a CAA, we can authenticate your identity documents in person and certify them for the IRS, so you never have to mail your originals anywhere. We also review your application for accuracy before submitting, which reduces the chance of delays or rejection.

How Long Does the ITIN Process Take?

ITIN applications submitted during peak tax season (January through April) typically take 9–11 weeks for the IRS to process. Outside of peak season, the timeline is usually 7 weeks. Once you receive your ITIN, it remains valid as long as you file a federal tax return at least once every three years. If you haven't filed for three consecutive years, your ITIN will expire and must be renewed.
